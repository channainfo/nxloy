## Overview

### What is Store Credit?

Store credit is a **monetary value issued by a business** that can be used by customers as payment for goods or services at that business. Store credit typically arises from:

- **Refunds**: Customer returns product, receives credit instead of cash refund
- **Promotional credits**: Business issues credit as incentive or compensation
- **Loyalty rewards**: Customer earns cashback/credit through loyalty program
- **Dispute resolution**: Credit issued to resolve customer complaints

### NxLoy's Positioning

**Critical Strategic Decision**: NxLoy structures store credit as **loyalty/rewards program benefits**, not as purchased gift cards.

**Why This Matters**:
- **ASEAN Advantage**: Loyalty programs receive favorable regulatory treatment across ASEAN
- **Philippines Exemption**: Exempted from strict Gift Check Act (no expiration prohibition)
- **Singapore Licensing**: No Payment Services Act licensing required for limited-purpose loyalty programs
- **Cambodia Simplicity**: Minimal regulation as rewards program vs. prepaid instrument
- **Financial Benefits**: No escheatment obligations (unredeemed balances stay with business)

### Regulatory Scope

Store credit regulations vary significantly by:

**ASEAN Markets** (NxLoy's Primary Operating Environment):
- **Cambodia**: Minimal regulation; general consumer protection laws apply
- **Singapore**: Limited-purpose e-money exempt from licensing; loyalty programs not regulated by MAS
- **Philippines**: Purchased gift cards heavily regulated; **loyalty programs exempt**
- **Vietnam, Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia**: Moderate consumer protection requirements

**US/EU Markets** (Reference for Global Expansion):
- **United States**: Extensive federal (CARD Act) and state-specific regulations
- **European Union**: Member state variation; no EU-wide harmonization
- **Key Difference**: US has complex escheatment laws; ASEAN does not

**Regulatory Factors**:
- **Jurisdiction**: ASEAN, US, EU laws differ significantly
- **Program Structure**: Loyalty program vs. purchased gift card (critical distinction)
- **Issuance Method**: Earned rewards vs. purchased with money
- **Value Characteristics**: Expiration, fees, transferability
- **Multi-Currency**: ASEAN multi-currency considerations

### Compliance Objectives

1. **Legal compliance**: Adhere to ASEAN regulations and best practices
2. **Consumer protection**: Transparent terms, fair treatment, clear disclosures
3. **Financial accuracy**: Proper accounting (IFRS/ASC 606), VAT/GST compliance
4. **Risk mitigation**: Prevent fraud, monitor regulatory changes
5. **Operational clarity**: Clear policies for staff, customers, and merchants
6. **Competitive advantage**: Leverage ASEAN regulatory benefits (no escheatment, loyalty exemptions)

## Legal Classification

### Store Credit vs. Gift Cards

**Critical Distinction**: The legal treatment of store credit differs from gift cards in many jurisdictions.

| Characteristic | Store Credit | Gift Card |
|----------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Purchase with money** | No (earned/issued) | Yes (bought as product) |
| **CARD Act applies** | Generally NO | YES (US federal law) |
| **State gift card laws** | Varies by state | Generally YES |
| **Expiration allowed** | More permissive | Highly restricted |
| **Fees allowed** | More permissive | Highly restricted |
| **Escheatment** | Varies | Often subject to |

### Types of Store Credit (Legal Perspective)

1. **Refund Credit**
   - Issued when customer returns merchandise
   - May be subject to store return policy
   - Some states require cash refund option for certain values

2. **Promotional Credit**
   - Issued as marketing incentive or compensation
   - Generally fewer restrictions than gift cards
   - More flexibility in expiration and terms

3. **Loyalty Reward Credit**
   - Earned through loyalty program participation
   - Often treated similarly to promotional credit
   - May have different tax treatment

#### Tax Treatment

**Value-Added Tax (VAT)**:
- Standard rate: **10%**
- Applied at redemption (not at credit issuance)

**Example**:
```
Customer earns $10 store credit (promotional reward)

Customer redeems $10 credit for $10 product

  - Customer pays: $1 cash (for VAT)
  OR
  - Customer uses $11 credit ($10 product + $1 VAT)
```

**Registration/Licensing**: Not required for limited-purpose store credit (business-specific)

**Risk Assessment**: **LOW**

**Recommended Actions**:

### Singapore - Primary Expansion

**Regulatory Authority**:
- Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS)
- Competition and Consumer Commission of Singapore (CCCS)

**Regulatory Status**: **Comprehensive payment services regulation with clear loyalty program exemption**

#### Payment Services Act 2019 (PS Act)

**Commencement**: January 28, 2020
**Latest Amendment**: April 4, 2024

**CRITICAL EXEMPTION FOR NXLOY**: Limited-Purpose E-Money Exemption

The PS Act **excludes** "limited purpose e-money" from licensing requirements if:
- E-money intended to be used **only in Singapore**
- Payment for goods/services provided by:
  - The issuer
  - Issuer's franchise group
  - Related corporations
- Stored value **cannot be withdrawn or refunded** in any currency

**Loyalty Programs Exclusion**:
MAS **does not intend to regulate** loyalty programmes common in retail:
- Designed to promote purchase of goods/services from issuer or specified merchants
- Do not pose same risks as general payment services

**NxLoy Qualification**:
- ‚úÖ Limited to NxLoy merchant network (limited purpose)
- ‚úÖ Non-withdrawable, non-refundable
- ‚úÖ Designed to promote merchant purchases (loyalty program)
- **Result**: **No MAS licensing required**

#### Consumer Protection (Fair Trading) Act

**Requirements**:
- No unfair practices
- Clear disclosure of terms
- Fair treatment in redemption
- Accessible customer service

#### Tax Treatment - Goods and Services Tax (GST)

**Standard Rate**: **9%** (effective January 1, 2024; increased from 8%)

**Multi-Purpose Voucher (MPV) Treatment**:
- GST charged when voucher is redeemed, not when issued
- Common approach for loyalty rewards

**Example**:
```
Customer earns $20 store credit
  - No GST at issuance

Customer redeems $20 credit for $20 product
  - Product value: $20
  - GST (9%): $1.80
  - Total: $21.80
  - Customer pays: $1.80 cash OR uses $21.80 credit
```

#### Compliance Requirements

**No MAS Licensing**: Confirmed (limited-purpose loyalty program exemption)

**Disclosures**:
- Terms and conditions accessible before participation
- Clear expiration, redemption rules, restrictions
- Customer service contact

**Record Keeping**:
- Transaction logs for audit
- GST accounting records
- Customer balance tracking

**Risk Assessment**: **LOW**

**Recommended Actions**:
- [ ] Confirm limited-purpose exemption with MAS (written guidance)
- [ ] Draft Consumer Protection (Fair Trading) Act compliant terms
- [ ] Implement MRV GST accounting (9%)
- [ ] Ensure non-withdrawal, non-refund policy enforced

### Philippines - Loyalty Exemption Strategy

**Regulatory Authority**:
- Department of Trade and Industry (DTI)
- Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) - for financial services

**Regulatory Status**: **Most restrictive for purchased gift cards; favorable for loyalty programs**

#### Republic Act No. 10962 - Gift Check Act of 2017

**Signed**: December 19, 2017

**Scope**: All issuers of gift checks, gift certificates, or gift cards (paper, card, code, or other device)

**Key Prohibitions** (for purchased gift cards):
1. Issuing gift checks with expiry dates
2. Imposing expiry date on stored value/credit/balance
3. Refusing to honor unused value/credit/balance

**Penalties**: PHP 500,000 to PHP 1,000,000 for violations

#### CRITICAL EXEMPTION (KEY FOR NXLOY)

**NOT Covered by Gift Check Act**:
1. **Loyalty, rewards, or promotional program gift checks** (as determined by DTI)
2. Coupons/vouchers for discounts or pre-identified goods

**DTI Administrative Order No. 10-04**:
- Gift checks distributed under award, loyalty, or promotional programs **NOT covered**
- DTI has exclusive jurisdiction to determine exemption eligibility

**NxLoy Strategy**: **MUST structure as loyalty/rewards program to qualify for exemption**

#### Compliance Requirements for NxLoy

**CRITICAL DECISION**: Structure as loyalty/rewards program (NOT general gift cards)

**Requirements for Exemption**:
1. **DTI Determination**: Loyalty/rewards programs are exempt
2. **Program Documentation**: Clear loyalty program structure
3. **Marketing**: Position as rewards, NOT purchased gift cards
4. **No Cash Sales**: Do NOT sell "gift cards" for money

**If Structured as Loyalty Program**:
- ‚úÖ Expiration allowed (reasonable terms, e.g., 12-24 months)
- ‚úÖ Fees allowed (though not recommended)
- ‚úÖ Clear terms of service permitted
- ‚úÖ No PHP 500k-1M penalty risk

**If Structured as General Gift Cards**:
- ‚ùå NO EXPIRATION allowed
- ‚ùå NO FEES on stored value
- ‚ùå MUST HONOR full unredeemed value indefinitely
- ‚ùå HIGH PENALTY RISK (PHP 500k-1M)

**Risk Assessment**: **HIGH IF GIFT CARDS / LOW IF LOYALTY PROGRAM**

**Recommended Actions**:
- [ ] Legal opinion from Philippines counsel on loyalty program classification
- [ ] DTI consultation on program structure (written confirmation)
- [ ] Clear documentation: Loyalty program, not gift card sales
- [ ] Marketing review: Avoid "gift card" language
- [ ] Terms of service: State "loyalty rewards, not gift checks"

## United States Regulations (Reference)

### Federal Level

#### Credit CARD Act of 2009

**Primary Law**: Credit Card Accountability Responsibility and Disclosure Act

**Applicability to Store Credit**:
- Generally **does NOT apply** to store credit unless credit is sold/marketed like a gift card
- **DOES apply** if:
  - Credit is sold for cash at point of sale
  - Credit is marketed as a gift product
  - Credit can be used at multiple unaffiliated merchants

**Key Provisions (if applicable)**:
- Minimum 5-year expiration from issuance or last load
- No dormancy/inactivity fees for 12 months
- Clear disclosure of terms and conditions
- No expiration of underlying funds (though access may expire)

#### Federal Trade Commission (FTC) Regulations

**Applicable Rules**:
- **Truth in Advertising**: Store credit terms must be clearly disclosed
- **Unfair/Deceptive Practices**: Cannot mislead customers about credit value, expiration, or restrictions
- **Electronic Fund Transfer Act**: If credit stored electronically, consumer protections apply

### State-Level Regulations

**Key Point**: State laws vary significantly. NxLoy must implement **jurisdiction-specific compliance**.

#### California (Most Restrictive)

**California Civil Code ¬ß 1749.5 (Gift Certificate Law)**

**Key Provisions**:
- **Store credit from refunds**: Subject to gift certificate law
- **Expiration prohibited**: Store credit cannot expire (with exceptions)
- **No fees**: Cannot charge dormancy, service, or other fees
- **Cash redemption**: Must provide cash for balances under $10
- **Exceptions**:
  - Credit issued for promotional/loyalty purposes (no purchase required) MAY have expiration
  - Credit from rewards programs may have different treatment

**Compliance Requirements**:
```
IF customer_location = "California" AND credit_source = "refund" THEN
  - expiration = NULL (cannot expire)
  - fees_allowed = FALSE
  - cash_redemption_threshold = $10.00
ELSE IF credit_source = "promotional" OR credit_source = "loyalty_reward" THEN
  - expiration_allowed = TRUE (with clear disclosure)
  - fees_allowed = FALSE (still restricted)
END IF
```

#### New York

**General Obligations Law ¬ß 5-1112**

**Key Provisions**:
- Gift certificates/store credit cannot expire for 5 years
- Applies to "gift certificates" broadly defined
- Promotional credit with no purchase required may be exempt

#### Massachusetts

**Chapter 255D: Regulation of Business Practices**

**Key Provisions**:
- No expiration on gift certificates/store credit
- Exception for promotional/free credit (must be clearly disclosed)
- No fees or charges allowed

#### Florida

**Florida Statute ¬ß 501.95**

**Key Provisions**:
- Store value may not decrease over time
- No fees unless disclosed at time of issuance
- Expiration allowed only if clearly disclosed

#### Texas

**Texas Business & Commerce Code ¬ß 35.47**

**Key Provisions**:
- Gift cards/certificates cannot expire for at least 3 years
- Promotional certificates may have shorter expiration with disclosure
- Service fees prohibited for 24 months

#### State Law Summary Matrix

## Expiration Rules

### When Expiration is Allowed

Based on multi-jurisdictional analysis:

**Permitted Expiration**:
1. **Promotional credit** (no purchase required)
2. **Loyalty reward credit** (earned, not purchased)
3. **Compensation credit** (issued for service recovery)
4. **Jurisdictions without specific restrictions**

**Prohibited Expiration**:
1. **Refund credit** in CA, MA, NY, WA
2. **Any credit** if marketed as "gift card"
3. **Purchased gift cards** (see gift card compliance doc)

### Minimum Expiration Periods (When Allowed)

| Jurisdiction | Minimum Duration | Notes |
|--------------|------------------|-------|
| **Federal (US)** | 5 years | If CARD Act applies |
| **California** | N/A | Expiration prohibited for refunds |
| **New York** | 5 years | Gift certificates broadly defined |
| **Texas** | 3 years | For gift cards/certificates |
| **Florida** | No minimum | Must be disclosed |
| **Australia** | 3 years | Gift cards only |
| **Canada (ON)** | N/A | Gift cards cannot expire |

### Recommended Implementation

**Safe Harbor Approach**:
```
Default Expiration Policy:
- Refund credit: NO EXPIRATION (safest compliance)
- Promotional credit: 12 months with clear disclosure
- Loyalty reward credit: 12 months with clear disclosure
- Allow business override: YES (with compliance warnings)

Disclosure Requirements:
- Expiration date shown at issuance
- Email/SMS reminder 30 days before expiration
- Visible in customer account dashboard
- Printed on physical vouchers (if applicable)
```

### Expiration Grace Periods

**Best Practice**:
- 30-day grace period after stated expiration
- Customer service can extend on case-by-case basis
- Log all expiration extensions for audit

## Financial Reporting Requirements

### Accounting Treatment

**Store Credit as Liability**

Store credit represents a **deferred revenue liability** on the business's balance sheet:

```
Journal Entry at Issuance:
  DR: Cash / Accounts Receivable (if refund) or Marketing Expense (if promotional)
  CR: Store Credit Liability

Journal Entry at Redemption:
  DR: Store Credit Liability
  CR: Revenue

Journal Entry at Expiration:
  DR: Store Credit Liability
  CR: Breakage Revenue
```

**ASEAN Context**:
- Same accounting treatment applies (IFRS 15 / ASC 606)
- **Major advantage**: No escheatment obligations = can recognize breakage more easily
- Cambodia businesses likely follow IFRS (check local GAAP)
- Singapore businesses must follow SFRS (aligned with IFRS)

### Breakage Revenue Recognition

**Breakage**: Unredeemed store credit that will likely never be redeemed

**Accounting Standards**:
- **IFRS 15** (International / ASEAN): Revenue from Contracts with Customers
- **ASC 606** (US GAAP): Revenue from Contracts with Customers

**Breakage Recognition Criteria**:
1. **Historical data**: Track historical redemption patterns
2. **Statistical modeling**: Estimate breakage based on age of credit
3. **Remote threshold**: Only recognize breakage when redemption is "remote"
4. **Legal compliance**: Cannot recognize breakage if expiration is illegal

**ASEAN Advantage for Breakage Recognition**:
- ‚úÖ **No escheatment conflicts**: Can recognize breakage without government remittance obligations
- ‚úÖ **Flexible expiration** (loyalty programs): Can set reasonable expiration to trigger breakage
- ‚úÖ **Simpler accounting**: No need to reserve for potential escheatment
- ‚úÖ **Better economics**: Breakage stays with business, not remitted to state

**Example Breakage Model (ASEAN)**:
```
Months Since Issuance | Redemption Likelihood | Breakage Estimate | Notes
0-3 months            | 70%                   | 30%               | Active customers
3-6 months            | 50%                   | 50%               | Moderate engagement
6-12 months           | 30%                   | 70%               | Low engagement
12+ months            | 10%                   | 90%               | Approaching expiration
24+ months (expired)  | 0%                    | 100%              | Full breakage recognition

Cambodia/Singapore: Can recognize breakage at expiration (12-24 months)
Philippines: Must use loyalty program structure to allow expiration
```

**Conservative Approach for NxLoy**:
```
Breakage Recognition Policy:
- Track redemption patterns for 6-12 months to establish baseline
- Recognize breakage using Remote Method:
  - At expiration (12 months + 30-day grace period)
  - Based on historical non-redemption rates
- Initial estimate: 15% breakage rate (industry benchmark)
- Review quarterly and adjust based on actual data
```

### Financial Statement Disclosure

**Required Disclosures (ASEAN)**:
1. **Balance sheet**: Total store credit liability by currency (USD, KHR, SGD, etc.)
2. **Income statement**: Breakage revenue (if recognized)
3. **Notes**: Accounting policy for store credit (IFRS 15 compliance)
4. **Notes**: Redemption patterns and breakage estimates
5. **Notes**: Expiration policy and customer notification process
6. **Notes**: Multi-currency considerations (if applicable)
7. **Notes**: NO escheatment obligations (ASEAN advantage)

**Example Financial Statement Note (Cambodia)**:
```
Note 10: Store Credit Liability

The Company operates a loyalty program in which customers earn store credit
through purchases and promotional activities. Store credit is recorded as a
deferred revenue liability and recognized as revenue upon redemption.

The Company recognizes breakage revenue on unredeemed store credit when the

As of December 31, 2025:
- Store credit liability:           $50,000 (KHR 200,000,000)
- Store credits issued (year):      $120,000
- Store credits redeemed (year):    $85,000

The Company is not subject to escheatment laws in Cambodia. Unredeemed balances

```

## Tax Implications

### ASEAN Tax Treatment (Primary Operating Environment)

**General Rule**: VAT/GST is collected **at redemption**, not at issuance

#### Cambodia - VAT Treatment (10%)

**Promotional Credit Issuance**:
```
Business issues $10 store credit (loyalty reward):
  - No VAT at issuance (not a sale)
  - Recorded as marketing expense or deferred revenue
```

**Redemption**:
```
Customer buys $50 product using $10 credit:
  - Product value: $50
  - VAT (10%): $5
  - Customer payment:
    Option A: $10 credit + $40 cash + $5 VAT = $55 total
    Option B: $11 credit (if credit covers VAT) + $44 cash = $55 total

Recommended: Option A (customer pays VAT separately)
- Clearer accounting
- Credit value = product value (not tax-inclusive)
```

**Refund Credit**:
```
Original Sale:
  - Customer pays $100 + $10 VAT = $110
  - Customer returns product
  - Refund: $100 store credit + $10 VAT refund (cash)

Redemption:
  - Customer uses $100 credit for new $100 product
  - VAT (10%): $10
  - Customer pays: $10 VAT (cash or additional credit if full refund)
```

**VAT Compliance**:
- VAT returns due by 20th of following month
- Store credit redemption = taxable supply
- Record VAT at redemption, not issuance

#### Singapore - GST Treatment (9%)

**Multi-Purpose Voucher (MPV) Classification**:
Store credit from loyalty programs typically treated as MPV:
- GST charged when voucher is **redeemed**, not when issued
- If loyalty credit expires unredeemed, no GST on breakage revenue

**Example**:
```
Customer earns $20 store credit:
  - No GST at issuance

Redemption:
  - Customer buys $20 product
  - GST (9%): $1.80
  - Total: $21.80
  - Payment: $20 credit + $1.80 cash (for GST)

OR (alternative):
  - Customer uses $21.80 credit (if system allows GST-inclusive credits)
```

**Breakage Revenue**:
```
Store credit expires unredeemed:
  - No GST on breakage revenue
  - Breakage = service that was never provided
  - Consult IRAS for specific guidance
```

### ASEAN Income Tax Treatment

**For Businesses**:

1. **Issuance**: Not taxable income (liability created)
2. **Redemption**: Revenue recognized and taxable when credit redeemed
3. **Breakage**: Taxable as income when recognized (major advantage: no escheatment offset)
4. **Promotional credit**: Deductible as marketing expense in most jurisdictions

**Cambodia Corporate Tax**:
- Rate: 20% on taxable income
- Breakage revenue = taxable income
- Marketing expenses (promotional credits) = deductible

**Singapore Corporate Tax**:
- Rate: 17% (standard), progressive rates for first SGD 300k
- Breakage revenue = taxable income
- GST-registered businesses follow MRV rules

**For Customers (Generally)**:

1. **Refund credit**: Not taxable (return of purchase price)
2. **Promotional credit**: Generally not taxable in ASEAN (treated as discount/rebate)
3. **Loyalty reward credit**: Not taxable (marketing incentive, not income)

**Key Difference from US**:
- ASEAN generally does NOT treat promotional rewards as taxable income to recipients
- No equivalent to US IRS Form 1099 requirements for $600+ promotional credits
- Simpler compliance for both business and customers

### US Sales Tax Treatment (Reference Only)

**General Rule**: Sales tax is collected **at redemption**, not at issuance

#### Refund Credit
```
Original Sale:
  - Customer pays $100 + $8 sales tax = $108
  - Customer returns product
  - Refund: $100 store credit + $8 tax refund OR $108 total credit (varies by state)

Redemption:
  - Customer uses $100 credit to buy new product
  - Sales tax calculated on $100 = $8
  - Customer pays $8 sales tax (or uses additional credit if full refund issued)
```

#### Promotional Credit
```

  - Business issues $10 promotional credit
  - No sales tax at issuance

Redemption:
  - Customer buys $50 product
  - Applies $10 credit

  - Customer pays: $50 - $10 credit + $4 tax = $44
```

## Escheatment & Unclaimed Property

**üéØ CRITICAL ASEAN ADVANTAGE**: **ASEAN countries have NO escheatment/unclaimed property laws**

This is a **major financial and operational advantage** for NxLoy operating in ASEAN markets compared to US/EU:
- **No obligation** to remit unredeemed balances to government
- **Simplified accounting**: Can recognize breakage revenue without escheatment complications
- **No compliance burden**: No annual reporting, due diligence, or remittance requirements
- **Better economics**: Unredeemed balances benefit the business, not the state

**This section applies ONLY to businesses serving US/EU customers**. For ASEAN operations, escheatment is **not applicable**.

---

### What is Escheatment? (US/EU Context)

**Definition**: Escheatment is the process by which unclaimed property is turned over to the state.

**Applicability** (US Only): Unredeemed store credit may be considered unclaimed property if:
1. Credit has been inactive for statutory period (3-5 years)
2. Business cannot locate customer
3. State law requires escheatment of store credits

**ASEAN Status**: **Not applicable** - No ASEAN country has escheatment laws

### State Escheatment Laws

**Dormancy Periods by State**:

| State | Dormancy Period | Store Credit Covered? | Notes |
|-------|----------------|----------------------|-------|
| **California** | 3 years | YES | Gift cards and unredeemed credits |
| **New York** | 5 years | YES | Gift certificates and credits |
| **Texas** | 5 years | Varies | Depends on credit type |
| **Florida** | 5 years | Varies | May exclude promotional credits |
| **Delaware** | 5 years | YES | Aggressively enforces |

**Exemptions**:
- Some states exempt promotional credits
- Some states exempt credits under de minimis threshold ($25-$50)
- Loyalty program credits may be exempt in some jurisdictions

### Escheatment Compliance Process

**Annual Requirements**:

1. **Due Diligence** (60-120 days before report due):
   - Attempt to contact customers with dormant credits
   - Email/postal mail to last known address
   - Document all contact attempts

2. **Reporting** (Typically November 1):
   - File unclaimed property report with each state
   - List customer names, addresses, and credit amounts
   - Submit electronically via state portal

3. **Remittance** (With report or by deadline):
   - Remit unredeemed credit amounts to state
   - States hold funds for customers to claim
   - Business no longer has liability

4. **Record Retention**:
   - Maintain escheatment records for 7-10 years
   - Provide to auditors on request

**Example Timeline**:
```
July 1: Identify all credits dormant for 3+ years (assuming CA rules)
August 1-31: Send due diligence letters to customers
September 1-30: Compile escheatment report
November 1: File report and remit funds to California
```

### Implementation in NxLoy

**Database Requirements**:
- Track `lastActivityDate` for each store credit
- Flag credits approaching dormancy period
- Store customer contact information for due diligence
- Generate escheatment reports by state

**Automated Workflows**:
- Monthly job: Identify dormant credits
- Quarterly job: Send reminder emails to inactive customers
- Annual job: Generate escheatment reports
- Integration with state reporting systems (e.g., NAUPA)
